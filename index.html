Tactical Meeting · Daily Huddle
Daily · 30 min · Company-wide

Your space to stop waiting.

This is a tactical meeting — not strategy, not roadmap, not debate. Thirty minutes a day, every lane reports their numbers and asks for what they need. If you're stuck on a video from Vishen, an approval, a decision — this is where you raise it and walk out unblocked. Not next week. Today. And when someone hits their number or ships something hard, the room cheers them on. Thumbs up, fire emojis, real energy. This is not a boring reporting meeting — it's the room that knows what's happening across the whole business and reacts to it together.

01

The meeting is yours.

The Daily Huddle isn't a status report you owe to leadership. It's a tool you use. Every owner has a slot. The slot exists so you can move work that's stuck, get decisions you need, and surface blockers before they cost you a week.

USE IT TOGet unstuck, today

If you need a video from Vishen, a decision from an exec, an approval from another team — raise it here. The right people are in the room. Walk out with a yes, a no, or a date. Not "I'll get back to you."

USE IT TOShow what's working — and what's not

Every owner reports their numbers daily. If you're on target with no blocker, pass in 10 seconds. If you're off target, this is where you say what you're doing today and this week to close the gap.

USE IT TOSolve problems live

Execs (CGO, CFO, CPO, CTO, CHRO) are in the room to make decisions, not to listen. When a blocker is raised, they solve it on the spot or commit to a same-day answer. Vishen gets a daily read of what's moving across the company.

USE IT TOCheer the wins

When someone hits a number, ships something hard, or unblocks a teammate — the room reacts. Thumbs up, fire emojis, "let's go." Real energy. This isn't a status sweep. We're a team that knows what each other is building and we celebrate when it lands.

USE IT TOLearn the heartbeat of the company

The Huddle is a learning environment. Even if you're an editor, a designer, a copywriter — come and learn. What's working. What's not. Which video performed yesterday. Which one flopped. What today's message is. You walk out understanding the campaign and the company you're working inside. If you don't get this signal, you work in a silo — and your work drifts from the core.

DON'T USE IT FORStrategy, roadmap, or long debates

This is tactical. Targets and roadmap belong to the weekly Revenue Task Force. If a topic needs more than two minutes of discussion, the owner books a follow-up meeting same-day or next-day and moves on.

02

How to report.

Crisp. Numbers, not story. Every reporter says the same four lines. If you're on target with nothing stuck, you pass in 10 seconds. If you're off target or stuck, this is where you say what's wrong and what you need.

Daily Huddle · Reporter Template
Lane: [Pathway / Function name]
Numbers: [Yesterday's KPIs · on target or off]
If off target: [Crisp diagnosis · "because X" · what we're doing today / this week]
Ask: [What I need from someone in this room · or "none"]
Manifesting Pathway. ROAS 75% vs 95% target — creative is fatigued. New VSL goes live today, second test tomorrow. Ask: need Brand approval on the new VSL by 1pm or we lose the day.
RULE 01

Numbers come from the dashboard

Lane owner updates Mission OS and the relevant dashboards by 8:00am. If it's not on the dashboard, you don't have a slot.

RULE 02

On target, no blocker = 10 seconds

"On target, no asks." Move on. Brevity is rewarded. The room earns time on the lanes that need it.

RULE 03

Off target = diagnosis + fix

"ROAS 75% because creative fatigued. Testing new VSL today." Not "we had a hard day, lots going on, working on it." Crisp. Two sentences max.

04

Who does what.

Daniel chairs because growth is his domain. Owners run their own slot. Execs solve.

Role 01

The Owner — runs their slot

You don't wait to be called. When the lane in front of you finishes, you go. Update your dashboard by 8am. Report crisp — numbers, diagnosis, fix, ask. If you need something from someone in the room, raise it. This is your space to stop waiting on people.

Role 02

Daniel — Chair (Head of Growth)

This is a tactical meeting and growth is Daniel's domain, so he chairs. He keeps time, cuts at 75 seconds, reads back the unsolved blockers at the end so owners book follow-ups. He also sets the energy — calls out the wins, leads the cheering, makes sure the room reacts to what's shipping. Not a passive timekeeper. The meeting feels like Daniel.

Role 03

The Exec — solves live

Marisha, Vishen, Dario, Norman, Alex, Jill — you're here to solve, not listen. When an owner raises a blocker that's yours to unblock, answer in the room. Yes, no, or a same-day commitment. The whole point of this meeting is owners stop waiting on you for days. Don't make them.

05

The rules that make this work.

These are the hygiene rules. Without them the meeting drifts back to a 60-minute parade. With them it stays at 30.

01The dashboard is the source

Numbers update by 8:00am. If they're not in Mission OS, you don't have a slot. The huddle reads the data; it doesn't generate it.

02Owners run their own slot

Everyone knows the order. When the lane before you finishes, you go. No one waits to be called. The meeting runs without the facilitator carrying it.

03On target + no blocker = 10 seconds

"On target, no asks." Pass. The room earns its time on the lanes that need attention.

04Off target = crisp diagnosis + crisp fix

"ROAS 75% because creative fatigued. New VSL today." Two sentences. Not a story. Not a hedge.

05Raise blockers here, not later

If you need a video, an approval, a decision — say it now. The right people are in the room. This meeting exists so you stop waiting days for things you could resolve in minutes.

06Execs solve live, or commit same-day

When a blocker is raised, the exec who can unblock it answers in the room. Yes, no, or a time before EOD. Not "I'll get back to you next week."

07Unsolved blockers → follow-up booked

Anything that needs more than two minutes of discussion gets a follow-up meeting. The owner who raised it books it — same day, or next day at the latest. Daniel reads back the list before close so owners commit.

0875 seconds, hard cap

Daniel cuts at 75. The clock is louder than the speaker. Strategy and roadmap go to the weekly Revenue Task Force, not here.

09Energy, not silence

When someone hits a number, ships something hard, or unblocks a teammate — the room reacts. Thumbs up, fire emojis, a quick "let's go." This is the company team huddle. Boring is the failure mode.
