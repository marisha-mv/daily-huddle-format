Tactical Meeting · Daily Huddle
Daily · 30 min · Company-wide

Your space to stop waiting.

This is a tactical reporting meeting — not strategy, not roadmap, not problem-solving. Thirty minutes a day, every lane reports their numbers and asks for what they need. Decisions get made in the room — fast. If you're stuck on a video from Vishen, an approval, a routing call — this is where you raise it and walk out with a yes, a no, or a name. If a blocker needs real discussion, you book a follow-up within 48 hours. You don't sit here and debate it. And when someone hits their number or ships something hard, the room cheers them on. Thumbs up, fire emojis, real energy. This is the room that knows what's happening across the whole business and reacts to it together.

01

The meeting is yours.

The Daily Huddle isn't a status report you owe to leadership. It's a tool you use, and it has three jobs: get unstuck, learn what's working, cheer the wins. Everything else is noise.

USE IT TOGet unstuck

If you need a video from Vishen, a decision from an exec, an approval from another team — raise it here. The right people are in the room. Two paths for any blocker, no third one: either the exec answers live (yes / no / a name), or the owner books a follow-up within 48 hours to go deeper. What you don't do is debate it in the huddle.

USE IT TOLearn what's working

Every owner reports their numbers. On target with no blocker → pass in 10 seconds. Off target → say what's wrong and what you're doing today to close the gap. This is also a learning environment — editors, designers, copywriters: come and learn what's hitting, what's flopping, what today's campaign is. If you don't get this signal, you work in a silo and your work drifts from the core.

USE IT TOCheer the wins

When someone hits a number, ships something hard, or unblocks a teammate — the room reacts. Thumbs up, fire emojis, "let's go." Real energy. We're a team that knows what each other is building and celebrates when it lands. Boring is the failure mode.

DON'T USE IT FORStrategy, roadmap, or long debates

This is a tactical reporting meeting, not a problem-solving one. Targets and roadmap belong to the weekly Revenue Task Force. If a topic needs more than two minutes — "I'm struggling with X, I need to walk you through it" — the owner books a follow-up within 48 hours. We don't sit here and debate it.

02

How to report.

Crisp. Numbers, not story. Every reporter says the same four lines. If you're on target with nothing stuck, you pass in 10 seconds. If you're off target or stuck, this is where you say what's wrong and what you need.

Daily Huddle · Reporter Template
Lane: [Pathway / Function name]
Numbers: [Yesterday's KPIs · on target or off]
If off target: [Crisp diagnosis · "because X" · what we're doing today / this week]
Ask: [What I need from someone in this room · or "none"]
Manifesting Pathway. ROAS 75% vs 95% target — creative is fatigued. New VSL goes live today, second test tomorrow. Ask: need Brand approval on the new VSL by 1pm or we lose the day.
RULE 01

Share your screen — show the source

When it's your slot, share your screen. Show your dashboard, your Airtable, your working files — whatever holds the actual numbers. The room sees what you see. No verbal-only reports.

RULE 02

On target, no blocker = 10 seconds

"On target, no asks." Move on. Brevity is rewarded. The room earns time on the lanes that need it.

RULE 03

Off target = diagnosis + fix

"ROAS 75% because creative fatigued. Testing new VSL today." Not "we had a hard day, lots going on, working on it." Crisp. Two sentences max.

No central read-back. Slots own their own follow-ups.

  • If a blocker needed real discussion, you said so in your slot — and you named the stakeholders for the 48-hour follow-up there. The huddle doesn't track who booked what; the owner does.
  • Routing calls — "bump it to Vishen to film," "Brand approves by 1pm," "Ramya catches this for Vishen" — happen verbally in the slot. The exec heard it; that's the contract. No calendar invite needed.
  • Last 3 minutes are buffer for an overrun, a missed win, or an urgent item. If everything finished early, the meeting ends early.
04

Who does what.

Daniel chairs because growth is his domain. Owners run their own slot. Execs solve.

Role 01

The Owner — runs their slot

You don't wait to be called. When the lane in front of you finishes, you go. Update your dashboard by 8am. Report crisp — numbers, diagnosis, fix, ask. If you need something from someone in the room, raise it. This is your space to stop waiting on people.

Role 02

Daniel — Chair (Head of Growth)

This is a tactical meeting and growth is Daniel's domain, so he chairs. He keeps time, cuts at 75 seconds, and sets the energy — calls out wins, leads the cheering, makes sure the room reacts to what's shipping. He does not read back blockers at the end. Owners name their own follow-ups inside their slot. Daniel keeps the rhythm; ownership stays with the lane.

Role 03

The Exec — removes blockers

Marisha, Vishen, Dario, Norman, Alex, Jill — you're here to remove blockers, not listen. When an owner raises one that's yours, answer in the room: yes, no, or a name. If it needs more than two minutes, you take the follow-up within 48 hours. The whole point of this meeting is owners stop waiting on you for days. Don't make them.

Role 04

Ramya — Vishen's catcher (only)

Ramya collects requests for Vishen — and Vishen only. A video to film, an approval, a routing call → goes through Ramya. She logs it in the meeting and gets it to him after. Whether Vishen is in the room or not, Vishen-asks route through Ramya. Other execs catch their own; Ramya is not a general blocker desk.

05

The rules that make this work.

These are the hygiene rules. Without them the meeting drifts back to a 60-minute parade. With them it stays at 30.

01Data comes from the dashboard

Numbers update by 8:00am. If they're not in Mission OS, you don't have a slot. The huddle reads the data; it doesn't generate it.

02Owners run their own slot

Everyone knows the order. When the lane before you finishes, you go. No one waits to be called. The meeting runs without the facilitator carrying it.

03Blockers → answered live, or 48-hour follow-up

Two paths, no third. Either the exec answers in the room — yes, no, a name — or the owner books a follow-up meeting within 48 hours to go deeper. We don't debate it in the huddle.

04Customize your update to what matters

Report what's happening in your department or what's moving your numbers — not your full task list. If it doesn't affect the trajectory, it doesn't belong in your slot.

05Keep it crisp — 75 seconds, hard cap

Numbers, diagnosis, fix, ask. Don't tell us everything you're working on. On target with no blocker → pass in 10 seconds. Off target → two sentences. Daniel cuts at 75.

06If you're out, your slot still reports

Every slot has a backup. PTO, sick, traveling — your manager or a direct report covers and gives the update. There is always an update. The slot doesn't go dark because someone's away.

07Vishen-asks go through Ramya

Anything that needs Vishen — film a video, approve, decide — Ramya catches and routes. Vishen-only. Other execs answer their own asks live; Ramya is not the catch-all blocker desk.
